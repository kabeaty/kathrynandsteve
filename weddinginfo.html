Saengerrunde Hall

Saengerrunde Hall was established in 1879 by Saengerrunde, a German singing society, and is a National Historic Site. It's also the oldest continually operating bowling alley in Austin, so be ready to bring your bowling skills at one of its six lanes!

Things to Do in Austin

If you find yourself with some extra time in Austin, there are a lot of cool things to check out. Below is a list we've compiled of some of our favorites that we recommend. Let us know if there's anything else you'd like to try while you're here, and we're happy to make suggestions!

Food

Barbecue

  • Stiles Switch BBQ. This is a laid-black place in North Austin with some delicious BBQ.

Tacos

  • Tacodeli. We get tacos from here most weekends. Our favorites are the Otto and the Jess Special.

  • Torchy's Tacos. We recommend the Fried Avocado if you go here.

Vegetarian

  • Bouldin Creek Cafe. Delicious vegetarian and vegan food in a local, socially conscious, artsy Austin kinda place.

Attractions

Barton Springs Pool

  • Barton Springs Pool. If it's hot in Austin, it's always possible to cool off with a dip in Barton Springs. Located downtown, this pool fed from underground springs has an average temp of 68-70 degrees.

Broken Spoke

  • Broken Spoke. This honky-tonk dance hall has been in Austin since 1964. There are dance lessons at 8pm Wednesday - Saturday that teach the basics needed to two-step to one of the great country bands they always have.

LBJ Presidential Library

  • LBJ Presidential Library. It takes a few hours to walk through and see the exhibits and historical documents, including letters to and from LBJ and a replica of LBJ's Oval Office.

South Congress

  • South Congress. Walk down South Congress Avenue, popping in and out of vintage stores and boutiques, Allens cowboy boots, Lucy in Disguise costume shop, and more. Plus, grab a bite at Home Slice Pizza, South Congress Cafe, Hopdoddy, or Guero's.
